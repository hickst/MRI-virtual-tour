MRI Operator's Console

Computer Workstation

The Syngo workstation computer is used to select, run, and edit scan sequences. Once data has been collected, it can be copied to a USB drive for transport. The large monitor near the ceiling mirrors the smaller console monitor and facilitates teaching and observation. The actual computer (black) is barely visible (in the MRI Operator's Console photo) in a cabinet under the desk, on the right.

Overhead Display

The large monitor near the ceiling mirrors the smaller console monitor and facilitates teaching and observation.

Fluids Injector Control & Display

This device displays information about and controls the Bracco injection system, located in the scanner room.

Model Name: BRACCO Injeneering EmpowerMR Contrast Injection System Display

Oscilloscope

The oscilloscope displays all three MRI scanner gradients (x, y, z) in realtime during a scan and thus facilitates the development of new sequences by Biomedical Engineers.

Model Name: Agilent DSO1024A Oscilloscope 200MHz 4-Channels

Desktop Microphone

The Shure microphone allows the MRI technician and researchers to easily talk to the participant, before and between scans. It is considered good practice to keep the partipant informed about what to expect and how long each scan will be.

Intercom / Stop Scan

The intercom allows personnel and participants to communicate during the scan. The red STOP button will stop the scan (if, for example, the participant is uncomfortable). The STOP button will also stop scanner table movement (this is occasionally useful if hair or clothing gets caught when the table is moving in or out of the scanner).

Siemens PMU (Physiological Measurement Unit)

These wireless physiological monitors are shown in their charging station. When attached to a participant, they gather data such as the participant's ECG, respiration, and pulse rate. This physiological data can be viewed at the DOT display (on the scanner) and on the Syngo workstation computer.

Phillips Physiological Monitor

A 19-inch wireless display connects to the Philips Expression MR400 physiological monitor in the scan room. It re-displays the scan participant's vital signs (e.g., blood pressure, pulse rate, pulse oxygen, CO2 level, etc.) to the MRI technician.

Model Name: Philips Expression IP5

Emergency Quench Box

The big red button (center) is used in a life-threatening emergency to vent all the helium from the scanner. This dire action dissipates the magnetic field, which will then reach a "safe" level after several minutes.

Only quench the scanner in a life-threatening situation! Quenching the scanner can destroy a million dollar machine.

The WARNING light (top middle) indicates if the helium level is too low.
The POWER light (green) indicates a satisfactory voltage supply.
The SYSTEM ON and SYSTEM OFF (blue) buttons can be used to perform a "hard reset" of the scanner system.

Scan Room Purge / Power Control Buttons

Should the breathable oxygen in the scanner room ever drop below safe levels, an alarm will sound. The PURGE button (left top) can be used to exhaust the bad air from the scanner room, bringing in fresh air.

The E-STOP button (left bottom) shuts down the power to the scanner in an emergency.

The large EPO knob (on the right) is used to control power to the entire MR system, in an emergency.
