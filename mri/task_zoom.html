Task Workstation

Audio/Visual Devices

The ROLLS MA251 (top) is an amplifier & mixer which mixes audio signals from three channels and outputs the audio to special (Sensimetric) headphones worn by the participant in the scanner. An additional channel (CH 1: MIC) can relay audio responses FROM the participant (for recording) using the noise-reducing fMRI microphone in the scanner.
  • CH 4: Music from an auxiliary audio plug (e.g. music from a phone).
  • CH 3: Alternate stimulus, from an optional laptop.
  • CH 2: Stimulus from the Task PC's sound card.
  • CH 1: Participant audio FROM the special Optoacoustics microphone.
Model Name: Rolls MA251 Mixer/Amplifier

The ATEN switch (bottom) sends video to the BOLDscreen display (behind the scanner) from either the Task PC or a researcher laptop. It also connects participant button boxes to either the Task PC or the researcher laptop, via a USB connection. Here the green light is on for the laptop input.

Model Name: ATEN KVMP Switch CS1762a

Audio Monitor

The J-S audio monitor allows researchers to monitor the audio mix (from the Rolls mixer) which is sent to headphones worn by the scan participant. If the fMRI microphone is used in the scanner, it is included in the audio mix and researchers are able to hear the participant speaking.

The audio mix can be heard on the weird, egg-shaped speaker on the top shelf. Turn it up to listen to the audio tasks that are playing or to hear the participant. Turn it down when the scanner is running (as the scanner is very loud).

Button Box Interface

The button box interface connects to the Task PC (or a laptop) via USB, and to a button box in the scanner via fiber optic cables that run through the patch panel. Participant responses are collected through the button boxes.

Model Name: Current Designs 932 Interface & Power Supply

A Button Box

Participants' responses are collected with button boxes. The boxes use delicate fiber optic cables which connect to the interface box at the task workstation. Each button sends a different digit to the interface box.

Model Name: one button box from Current Designs Button Box Package 904

fMRI Microphone

The fMRI microphone, in the scanner, provides three output channels:
1) one channel records voice and scanner noise,
2) another channel records just the scanner noise, and
3) the third channel provides the real-time, noise-cancelled data using the other two channels.

The optiMRI software can parse these three channels to optimize speech and minimize scanner noise. Use channel 1 on the Rolls MA251 to control audio volume from the fMRI microphone.

Model Name: OptoAcoustics FOMRI III Dual Channel Microphone System for fMRI

Eye Tracking Computer

This computer is connected to the eye tracking camera at the back of the scanner to support MRI-compatible eye tracking experiments.
The Eyelink computer is also connected to the Task PC by an ethernet cable allowing custom software running on the Task PC to synchronize with the Eyelink system (which is helpful for adjusting camera position and focus).

Model Name: Eyelink 1000 Eye Tracking Computer

Task Workstation Computer

This is a computer provided to researchers for presenting experimental tasks to a participant in the MRI scanner. It has some software for stimulus presentation. However, most researchers bring their own laptops with dedicated software for their particular experiments. (Note that the actual computer is the black box under the desk, on the left.)

See Also: laptop docking station (Dock button).

Model Name: Lenovo Thinkstation 360 and ThinkVision LT2452p 24-inch 1920x1200 LED monitor.

Laptop Connection Cables

A laptop can be connected at the fMRI task workstation and used to present stimuli, administer experiments, or play media. (A separate laptop with your own custom task setup is often a better choice than the provided task computer).

On the desk, to the right of the Task PC, are several cables for connecting the laptop to the other task workstation components:
  • an HDMI cable, for presenting visual stimuli,
  • an audio cable with microphone (pink) and speaker (green) jacks, for audio inputs, and
  • a USB cable, for interfacing with the button boxes.

Injector Power Supply
