Task Workstation

Audio/Visual Devices

The ROLLS MA251 (top) is an amplifier & mixer which mixes audio signals from three channels and outputs the audio to headphones worn by the participant in the scanner. An additional channel (CH 1 - MIC) can collect audio responses FROM the participant for recording using a special noise-reducing microphone in the scanner.
  • CH 4: Music from an auxiliary audio plug (e.g. music from a phone).
  • CH 3: Alternate stimulus, from an optional laptop.
  • CH 2: Stimulus from the Task PC's sound card.
  • CH 1: Patient audio FROM the special Optoacoustics microphone.
Model Name: Rolls MA251 Mixer/Amplifier

The ATEN switch (bottom) selects video from either the Task PC or a laptop. Here the green light is on for the right input (the laptop).

Model Name: ATEN KVMP Switch CS1762a

Audio Monitor

The J-S speaker allows researchers to hear something. Turn it up to hear the participant, or to ensure that auditory tasks are playing. Turn it down when the scanner is running (the scanner is very loud).

Button Box Interface

The button box interface connects to the Task PC via USB, and to a group of button boxes at the MRI via fiber optic cables which run through the patch panel. Participant responses may be collected through the button boxes.

Model Name: Current Designs 932 Interface & Power Supply (interfaces to the Current Designs Button Box Package 904 in the scanner room).

Task Workstation Computer

This is a computer provided to researchers for presenting experimental tasks to a participant in the MRI scanner. It has some software for stimulus presentation. However, most researchers bring their own laptops with dedicated software for their particular experiments. (Note that the actual computer is under the desk, on the left.)

See Also: laptop docking station (Dock button).

Model Name: Lenovo Thinkstation 360 and ThinkVision LT2452p 24-inch 1920x1200 LED monitor.
